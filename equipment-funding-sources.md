## Federal Funding Sources

### NSF Major Research Instrumentation (MRI)

- **Agency:** National Science Foundation
- **Award range:** $100K–$4M (Track 1: $100K–$1.4M; Track 2: $1.4M–$4M; Track 3: $100K–$4M for helium conservation)
- **Cost sharing:** Currently waived through 2026 (CHIPS and Science Act)
- **Institutional limit:** Up to 4 proposals per institution per competition (2 Track 1, 1 Track 2, 1 Track 3)
- **Deadline:** Next submission window expected Oct 15–Nov 16, 2026
- **Relevance:** Well-suited for multi-user equipment like LPBF printers, atomizers, and indentation plastometry systems. Proposals emphasizing broad institutional impact and student training are competitive.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/mri-major-research-instrumentation-program

### DoD Defense University Research Instrumentation Program (DURIP)

- **Agency:** Department of Defense (Army, Navy, Air Force research offices)
- **Award range:** $50K–$3M per instrument
- **FY25 total:** $43M awarded to 112 researchers at 64 institutions
- **FY26 deadline:** April 25, 2025
- **Focus areas:** Novel materials, quantum, photonics, autonomy, and defense-relevant research
- **Relevance:** LPBF printers and atomizers for defense-relevant alloy development (e.g., lightweight structural alloys, high-temperature materials). Equipment must support defense-relevant basic research.
- **Note:** Cannot fund construction, building systems, fixed equipment (e.g., clean rooms), or salaries
- **Link:** https://www.onr.navy.mil/work-with-us/funding-opportunities/fiscal-year-fy-2026-department-defense-dod-defense-university

### NSF EPSCoR (Utah is eligible)

Utah is an [EPSCoR-eligible jurisdiction](https://utepscor.org/about.html) through at least FY2027 (frozen by CHIPS and Science Act).

- **E-CORE (Collaborations for Optimizing Research Ecosystems):** Focuses on development of research infrastructure and sustainable research capacity
- **RII-FEC (Focused EPSCoR Collaborations):** Multi-institutional, multi-state collaborations; requires at least two eligible jurisdictions
- **Relevance:** Infrastructure improvement grants can include major equipment acquisition. EPSCoR status may provide additional proposal opportunities and favorable review considerations.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/e-core-epscor-research-infrastructure-improvement-program-epscor

### DOE EPSCoR

- **Agency:** Department of Energy, Office of Science
- **Focus:** Build research infrastructure in EPSCoR-eligible states for energy-related research
- **Relevance:** Alloy development for energy applications (e.g., fusion, nuclear, lightweight structures) aligns with DOE priorities. Equipment for powder production and materials characterization may qualify.
- **Link:** https://science.osti.gov/bes/epscor

### NSF Advanced Manufacturing (AM)

- **Agency:** NSF, Division of Civil, Mechanical and Manufacturing Innovation (CMMI)
- **Focus:** Transformative research in manufacturing processes, systems, machines, and materials processing including additive manufacturing
- **Relevance:** LPBF printer and ultrasonic atomizer research that advances fundamental understanding of manufacturing processes. May support equipment within research proposals.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/am-advanced-manufacturing

### NSF Materials Engineering and Processing (MEP)

- **Agency:** NSF CMMI
- **Focus:** Relationship between processing methods (including additive manufacturing) and material structure, properties, and performance
- **Relevance:** Directly relevant to alloy powder processing (atomization) and LPBF manufacturing. Supports analytical, experimental, and numerical characterization.
- **Link:** https://simpler.grants.gov/opportunity/359617

### NSF Materials Innovation Platforms (MIP)

- **Agency:** NSF Division of Materials Research (DMR)
- **Focus:** Mid-scale infrastructure funding for advanced materials research tools; 2025 competition focused on alloys, amorphous, and composite materials
- **Relevance:** Directly aligned with alloy workflow. Could support atomizer and LPBF equipment as part of a materials platform proposal.
- **Link:** https://nsf-gov-resources.nsf.gov/files/nsf25521.pdf

### NSF Future Manufacturing (FM)

- **Agency:** NSF
- **Award range:** Up to $3M/4 years (research grants); ~$500K/2 years (seed grants)
- **FY25 investment:** $25.5M across 7 major grants and 9 seed projects at 36 institutions
- **Focus:** Pioneering new manufacturing paradigms including additive manufacturing, robotics, digital twins, and advanced materials
- **Relevance:** Alloy workflow combining atomization, LPBF, and automated characterization (PIP) fits the paradigm of next-generation manufacturing.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/fm-future-manufacturing

### DOE Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO)

- **Agency:** Department of Energy, EERE
- **Focus:** R&D in advanced materials and manufacturing, including additive manufacturing
- **Relevance:** Powder metallurgy, additive manufacturing, and materials characterization for energy applications
- **Link:** https://www.energy.gov/eere/ammto/ammto-funded-projects

### America Makes (National Additive Manufacturing Innovation Institute)

- **Type:** Public-private partnership / Manufacturing USA institute
- **Focus:** Additive manufacturing technology development, qualification, and workforce
- **Recent calls:** QTIME ($5M for NDI in AM); AM-CPC (cross-platform consistency, ~$250K); JAQS-SQ (LPBF supplier qualification, 2026)
- **Relevance:** LPBF-specific research funding. Universities eligible to compete alongside industry. Collaborative consortia preferred.
- **Link:** https://www.americamakes.us/opportunities/

### NSF DMR Topical Materials Research Programs (TMRP)

- **Agency:** NSF Division of Materials Research
- **Focus:** Biomaterials, ceramics, electronic/photonic materials, metals, polymers, and materials chemistry
- **Relevance:** Metals-focused proposals for alloy development. Equipment may be included as part of broader research proposals. Watch for Dear Colleague Letters announcing instrumentation supplements.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/dmr-tmrp-division-materials-research-topical-materials-research

## Other Potential Sources

### NIST Office of Advanced Manufacturing (OAM)

- **Focus:** Cost-shared grants for digital manufacturing, AI, energy efficiency, and resilient supply chains
- **Relevance:** University-industry partnerships for advanced manufacturing testbeds
- **Link:** https://www.nist.gov/oam/funding-opportunities

### NSF SBIR/STTR

- **Note:** Requires a small business partner (STTR requires formal university collaboration)
- **Relevance:** Could fund development of novel atomization or characterization equipment in partnership with a small business
- **Link:** https://seedfund.nsf.gov/

### DOE SBIR/STTR

- **Note:** Similar to NSF SBIR/STTR; requires small business partner
- **Focus areas:** Advanced manufacturing, powder metallurgy, additive manufacturing for energy applications
- **Note:** Program authorization expires Sept 30, 2025; check for reauthorization status
- **Link:** https://www.energy.gov/science/sbir

## Notes

- **BYU EPSCoR eligibility:** Utah is EPSCoR-eligible for NSF, DOE, NASA, and USDA through at least FY2027, which opens additional funding opportunities and may strengthen proposals.
- **Alloy workflow narrative:** A compelling proposal could frame the three equipment pieces (atomizer → LPBF printer → PIP tester) as an integrated alloy development and characterization pipeline, connecting powder production, additive manufacturing, and rapid mechanical testing.
- **ASTM E3499-25:** The recent ASTM standardization of PIP testing (2025) strengthens the case for PIP equipment in grant proposals.
- **Multi-user access:** NSF MRI and similar programs favor equipment that serves multiple users, departments, and institutions. Emphasize cross-departmental and cross-institutional usage.
- **Cost sharing:** While NSF MRI cost-sharing is waived through 2026, other programs may require cost-sharing or matching funds. Check individual solicitations.
