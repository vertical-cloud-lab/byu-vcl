## Federal Funding Sources

### NSF Major Research Instrumentation (MRI)

- **Agency:** National Science Foundation
- **Award range:** $100K–$4M (Track 1: $100K–$1.4M; Track 2: $1.4M–$4M; Track 3: $100K–$4M for helium conservation)
- **Cost sharing:** Currently waived through 2026 (CHIPS and Science Act)
- **Institutional limit:** Up to 4 proposals per institution per competition (2 Track 1, 1 Track 2, 1 Track 3)
- **Deadline:** Next submission window expected Oct 15–Nov 16, 2026
- **PI eligibility:** **Assistant professors can serve as sole PI**, but equipment must serve a broad multi-user base (not single-lab use). Proposals must describe multiple research projects and investigators. This is a limited-submission program — institutions run internal competitions to select up to 4 proposals.
- **Relevance:** Well-suited for multi-user equipment like LPBF printers, atomizers, and indentation plastometry systems. Proposals emphasizing broad institutional impact and student training are competitive.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/mri-major-research-instrumentation-program

### DoD Defense University Research Instrumentation Program (DURIP)

- **Agency:** Department of Defense (Army, Navy, Air Force research offices)
- **Award range:** $50K–$3M per instrument
- **Cost sharing:** None required
- **FY25 total:** $43M awarded to 112 researchers at 64 institutions
- **FY26 deadline:** **April 25, 2025, 5:00 PM ET** (proposals to ONR, ARO, or AFOSR via Grants.gov)
- **PI eligibility:** No rank restriction — **assistant professors can serve as sole PI**. PI must be eligible per their institution's policies (typically tenure-track faculty). A PI may submit to more than one DoD service but may only accept funding from one.
- **Focus areas:** Novel materials, quantum, photonics, autonomy, and defense-relevant research
- **Relevance:** LPBF printers and atomizers for defense-relevant alloy development (e.g., lightweight structural alloys, high-temperature materials). Equipment must support defense-relevant basic research.
- **Note:** Cannot fund construction, building systems, fixed equipment (e.g., clean rooms), or salaries
- **Links:**
  - ONR: https://www.onr.navy.mil/work-with-us/funding-opportunities/fiscal-year-fy-2026-department-defense-dod-defense-university
  - Grants.gov: https://simpler.grants.gov/opportunity/358449

### NSF EPSCoR (Utah is eligible)

Utah is an [EPSCoR-eligible jurisdiction](https://utepscor.org/about.html) through at least FY2027 (frozen by CHIPS and Science Act).

- **E-CORE (Collaborations for Optimizing Research Ecosystems):**
  - Focuses on development of research infrastructure and sustainable research capacity
  - **Deadline:** July 15, 2025 (full proposal; annually on 3rd Tuesday in July)
  - **PI eligibility:** Jurisdiction-driven, institutional proposals — **not suitable for sole PI**. Typically led by state EPSCoR offices or jurisdictional government units.
  - **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/e-core-epscor-research-infrastructure-improvement-program-epscor/nsf25-523/solicitation
- **RII-FEC (Focused EPSCoR Collaborations):**
  - Multi-institutional, multi-state collaborations; requires at least two EPSCoR-eligible jurisdictions
  - **Deadline:** January 29, 2026 (full proposal; annually on 4th Tuesday in January)
  - **PI eligibility:** Requires interjurisdictional teams — **not suitable for sole PI**. Must have PI/Co-PI from each jurisdiction. An investigator may serve as PI on only one active RII-FEC award.
  - **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/epscor-research-infrastructure-improvement-program-focused/nsf24-573/solicitation
- **Relevance:** Infrastructure improvement grants can include major equipment acquisition. EPSCoR status may provide additional proposal opportunities and favorable review considerations. An assistant professor could participate as PI or Co-PI within a multi-institution team.

### DOE EPSCoR

- **Agency:** Department of Energy, Office of Science
- **Focus:** Build research infrastructure in EPSCoR-eligible states for energy-related research
- **FY26 deadline (State/National Lab Partnerships):** Pre-application March 18, 2026; full application **May 21, 2026**
- **PI eligibility:** Early career faculty (including assistant professors) are encouraged to apply. **Sole PI is permitted.** Collaboration with DOE National Laboratories encouraged.
- **Relevance:** Alloy development for energy applications (e.g., fusion, nuclear, lightweight structures) aligns with DOE priorities. Equipment for powder production and materials characterization may qualify.
- **Links:**
  - Program: https://science.osti.gov/bes/epscor
  - How to apply: https://science.osti.gov/bes/epscor/How-to-Apply

### NSF Advanced Manufacturing (AM)

- **Agency:** NSF, Division of Civil, Mechanical and Manufacturing Innovation (CMMI)
- **Focus:** Transformative research in manufacturing processes, systems, machines, and materials processing including additive manufacturing
- **Relevance:** LPBF printer and ultrasonic atomizer research that advances fundamental understanding of manufacturing processes. May support equipment within research proposals.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/am-advanced-manufacturing

### NSF Materials Engineering and Processing (MEP)

- **Agency:** NSF CMMI
- **Focus:** Relationship between processing methods (including additive manufacturing) and material structure, properties, and performance
- **Relevance:** Directly relevant to alloy powder processing (atomization) and LPBF manufacturing. Supports analytical, experimental, and numerical characterization.
- **Link:** https://simpler.grants.gov/opportunity/359617

### NSF Materials Innovation Platforms (MIP)

- **Agency:** NSF Division of Materials Research (DMR)
- **Focus:** Mid-scale infrastructure funding for advanced materials research tools; 2025 competition focused on alloys, amorphous, and composite materials
- **Relevance:** Directly aligned with alloy workflow. Could support atomizer and LPBF equipment as part of a materials platform proposal.
- **Link:** https://nsf-gov-resources.nsf.gov/files/nsf25521.pdf

### NSF Future Manufacturing (FM)

- **Agency:** NSF
- **Award range:** Up to $3M/4 years (research grants); ~$500K/2 years (seed grants)
- **FY25 investment:** $25.5M across 7 major grants and 9 seed projects at 36 institutions
- **Focus:** Pioneering new manufacturing paradigms including additive manufacturing, robotics, digital twins, and advanced materials
- **Relevance:** Alloy workflow combining atomization, LPBF, and automated characterization (PIP) fits the paradigm of next-generation manufacturing.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/fm-future-manufacturing

### DOE Advanced Materials and Manufacturing Technologies Office (AMMTO)

- **Agency:** Department of Energy, EERE
- **Focus:** R&D in advanced materials and manufacturing, including additive manufacturing
- **Relevance:** Powder metallurgy, additive manufacturing, and materials characterization for energy applications
- **Link:** https://www.energy.gov/eere/ammto/ammto-funded-projects

### America Makes (National Additive Manufacturing Innovation Institute)

- **Type:** Public-private partnership / Manufacturing USA institute
- **Focus:** Additive manufacturing technology development, qualification, and workforce
- **Recent calls:** QTIME ($5M for NDI in AM); AM-CPC (cross-platform consistency, ~$250K); JAQS-SQ (LPBF supplier qualification, 2026)
- **Relevance:** LPBF-specific research funding. Universities eligible to compete alongside industry. Collaborative consortia preferred.
- **Link:** https://www.americamakes.us/opportunities/

### NSF DMR Topical Materials Research Programs (TMRP)

- **Agency:** NSF Division of Materials Research
- **Focus:** Biomaterials, ceramics, electronic/photonic materials, metals, polymers, and materials chemistry
- **Relevance:** Metals-focused proposals for alloy development. Equipment may be included as part of broader research proposals. Watch for Dear Colleague Letters announcing instrumentation supplements.
- **Link:** https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/dmr-tmrp-division-materials-research-topical-materials-research

## Notes

- **BYU EPSCoR eligibility:** Utah is EPSCoR-eligible for NSF, DOE, NASA, and USDA through at least FY2027, which opens additional funding opportunities and may strengthen proposals.
- **Alloy workflow narrative:** A compelling proposal could frame the three equipment pieces (atomizer → LPBF printer → PIP tester) as an integrated alloy development and characterization pipeline, connecting powder production, additive manufacturing, and rapid mechanical testing.
- **ASTM E3499-25:** The recent ASTM standardization of PIP testing (2025) strengthens the case for PIP equipment in grant proposals.
- **Multi-user access:** NSF MRI and similar programs favor equipment that serves multiple users, departments, and institutions. Emphasize cross-departmental and cross-institutional usage.
- **Cost sharing:** While NSF MRI cost-sharing is waived through 2026, other programs may require cost-sharing or matching funds. Check individual solicitations.

## Recommendation: DURIP vs. DOE EPSCoR for BYU VCL Alloy Workflow

Both DoD DURIP and DOE EPSCoR are strong sole-PI options for an early career assistant professor. Here is how they compare for the specific BYU VCL alloy workflow (atomizer → LPBF → PIP).

| Factor | DoD DURIP | DOE EPSCoR (State/National Lab) |
|---|---|---|
| **Award amount** | $50K–$3M (equipment only) | $600K–$1M over 4 years (research + equipment) |
| **Funds equipment?** | ✅ Yes — entire purpose | ✅ Yes — within a research proposal |
| **Funds personnel/research?** | ❌ No — equipment only, no salaries | ✅ Yes — supports students, postdocs, travel |
| **Cost sharing** | None | None |
| **Next deadline** | FY27 cycle (likely spring 2026; FY26 closed April 25, 2025) | Pre-app **March 18, 2026**; full app **May 21, 2026** |
| **Early career emphasis** | No special preference, but no rank restriction | ✅ Explicitly encourages early career faculty |
| **Utah EPSCoR advantage** | N/A | ✅ Utah is EPSCoR-eligible through FY2027 — limited window |
| **Research framing** | Defense-relevant: lightweight structural alloys, high-temp materials, armor | Energy-relevant: alloys for nuclear/fusion, lightweight vehicles, grid/battery materials |
| **Collaboration required** | None | DOE National Lab partnership encouraged |
| **Est. success rate** | ~10–20% (112 awards from $43M in FY25) | Competitive (~39 awards from $36M in FY24) |
| **Award duration** | 1 year (equipment purchase) | Up to 4 years |

### Which to prioritize

**DOE EPSCoR is the stronger near-term option** for the BYU VCL alloy workflow because:

1. **Deadline is imminent** — pre-app March 18, 2026; full app May 21, 2026. DURIP FY26 has closed and FY27 is TBD.
2. **Utah EPSCoR eligibility is time-limited** — frozen through FY2027 by CHIPS Act, but could change. This is a strategic advantage to use now.
3. **Broader funding scope** — covers equipment, student support, and research costs in one proposal, vs. DURIP which is equipment-only with no personnel support.
4. **Early career encouragement** — the program explicitly encourages early career faculty applications.
5. **Natural energy framing** — the alloy workflow (custom powder → LPBF → rapid mechanical characterization) maps well to DOE priorities in advanced materials for energy (nuclear structural materials, lightweight transportation alloys, grid infrastructure).
6. **Self-driving lab angle** — autonomous materials discovery and high-throughput characterization align with DOE's interest in accelerating the materials development cycle.
7. **National Lab partnership** — collaboration with a DOE lab (e.g., ORNL MDF, Sandia, PNNL) strengthens the proposal and provides access to complementary capabilities.

**DoD DURIP remains excellent as a complementary or follow-up option** because:

1. **Larger equipment budgets** — up to $3M specifically for equipment, vs. equipment being a portion of a $600K–$1M DOE EPSCoR award.
2. **No collaboration required** — sole PI, no National Lab partnership needed.
3. **Strong defense framing** — alloy development for defense applications (lightweight armor, high-temp turbine materials, corrosion-resistant naval alloys) is a natural fit.
4. **Can be submitted in parallel** — DURIP and DOE EPSCoR are from different agencies, so both can be pursued simultaneously when the DURIP FY27 cycle opens.

### Recommended strategy

1. **Now (March 2026):** Submit DOE EPSCoR pre-application by March 18, 2026. Frame alloy workflow as energy-relevant materials discovery pipeline with National Lab collaboration.
2. **Spring 2026:** Develop full DOE EPSCoR proposal for May 21, 2026 deadline.
3. **When FY27 DURIP opens (~early 2026/2027):** Submit DURIP proposal for specific equipment (e.g., LPBF printer or atomizer) framed for defense-relevant alloy research.
4. **Fall 2026:** Consider NSF MRI for the full integrated pipeline if selected through BYU's internal limited-submission competition.
