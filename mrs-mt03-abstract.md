# MRS Abstract — MT03: AI-Driven Workflows and Autonomous Platforms for Functional Material Design and Catalysis

- **Format:** Invited talk (30 min, including questions)
- **Character limit:** 4,000
- **Tracking issue:** [#158](https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/byu-vcl/issues/158)

## Title

> Agentic Materials Research in the Era of AI: Insights from the Vertical Cloud Lab @ BYU

## Abstract

> Agents and large language models (LLMs) are reshaping research workflows, whether for literature search, topic exploration, teaching and learning, code development, manuscript and proposal writing, administrative work, control of robotic systems, or direct usage within materials optimization campaigns. In our lab, these tools are not occasional conveniences; they are core infrastructure for designing, building, and operating self-driving labs and the software ecosystems that surround them.
>
> We highlight our lab's use of these tools in four materials research applications. First, a self-driving lab for critical-mineral-lean, 3D-printable metal alloy powder for aerospace (https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/digital-alloy-lab), where agentic workflows support thermodynamic screening of candidate compositions, experiment planning, and closed-loop iteration between powder preparation, printing, and characterization. Second, generative systems prototyping for a low-cost, high-performance powder multi-dosing system (https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/powder-doser), in which LLM-assisted design iteration spans mechanical design, firmware, and control software. Third, a self-driving lab for mechanical performance optimization of tensegrity-inspired, multi-material structures for planetary landers (https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/tensegrity-optimization), combining additive manufacturing, automated mechanical testing, and Bayesian optimization over a mixed design space. Fourth, a self-driving lab for studying precipitate-formation reactions on inner walls of oil pipelines (https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/digital-wetlab), emphasizing sample-efficient experimental data collection for the relevant solution chemistry.
>
> Across all four applications, our workflows are tightly integrated with GitHub, leveraging GitHub Copilot and Claude Code GitHub Actions using frontier models. Issues and pull requests trigger parallelized multi-agent workflows, so that agent contributions—code, analyses, documentation, and design iterations—are captured with full visibility and provenance in version-controlled history and are subject to human review before merging. This repository-as-lab-notebook pattern lets students and collaborators delegate well-scoped tasks to agents, work asynchronously alongside them, and audit exactly what was done and why. It also lowers the barrier to entry for new researchers, who can contribute meaningfully to hardware, software, and campaign design earlier in their training.
>
> We also share the practical lessons and challenges behind these successes: verifying agent-generated code and analyses and guarding against plausible-but-wrong outputs; deciding which tasks to delegate versus keep human-led; maintaining safety and human oversight when agents interface with physical hardware; managing costs, rate limits, and rapidly evolving tooling; and teaching agentic development practices to new researchers. As agentic lifestyles normalize in science, we are excited to share our successes and challenges in accelerating materials discovery and commercialization.
