## Roadmap-risk assessment for the top 3

### 1. Next Generation Electrometallurgical Technologies

**Risk:** Moderate. The scope is ideal, but the audience will know electrochemical cells, electrodes, electrolytes, impurity tolerance, and current/potential control. If no potentiostat module exists by July 2026, an abstract that sounds like demonstrated electrowinning/electrorefining would overclaim.

**Honest wording:** “We present a modular SDL architecture for aqueous materials chemistry and outline an electrochemistry module under integration, coupling electrode handling and potentiostat control to closed-loop electrolyte and process screening.” Avoid “we demonstrate autonomous electrowinning” unless you actually have it.

### 2. AI-Enabled Materials Processing

**Risk:** Low to moderate. This symposium values workflow architecture, closed-loop control, and accelerated experimental processing, so a platform talk can stand even if electrochemistry is an extension. The risk is weaker electrochemistry specificity, not overclaiming.

**Honest wording:** “We demonstrate the platform’s current liquid/powder/sensing modules and describe how the same CubOS protocol and provenance layer extends to potentiostat-controlled electrodeposition and electrolyte-screening workflows.”

### 3. Rare Metal Extraction & Processing

**Risk:** Moderate. Current CubXL capabilities already fit aqueous leaching/separation studies, but not electrochemical separation unless a potentiostat/electrode module is built or at least credibly prototyped. The proceedings/journal note also means the work may need more technical substance than a pure roadmap abstract.

**Honest wording:** “Current modules support automated aqueous leaching and pH-controlled screening; planned electrochemical modules will enable vial-scale electrodialysis/electrowinning parameter studies with full experimental provenance.”

## Bottom line

**Single best-fit symposium:** **Next Generation Electrometallurgical Technologies for Metal Extraction, Refining, and Recycling**. It is the only venue where electrochemical extraction/refining/recycling, advanced electrolytes, closed-loop design, process control, and modularization all appear together in the scope.

**Backup 1:** **AI-Enabled Materials Processing: Integrating Accelerated Experimental Workflows and Processing-Aware Machine Learning**. It is the best SDL/closed-loop/AI venue and can accept electrodeposition or electrochemical surface modification as a processing route.

**Backup 2:** **Rare Metal Extraction & Processing**. It is the best aqueous-to-electrochemical extraction venue after the electrometallurgy symposium, especially for rare-metal recovery, electrochemical separations, and hydrometallurgical screening.

The electrochemistry framing **does open meaningfully better venues** than the current aqueous-chemistry framing, especially the Hydrometallurgy and Electrometallurgy Committee symposia. But unless a minimal potentiostat/electrode integration can be built before submission, I would not make the whole abstract sound like a completed electrochemistry SDL. Best practical move: submit an electrometallurgy-forward version that presents demonstrated CubXL/CubOS aqueous automation, then honestly says future or initial modules include potentiostat-controlled electrode handling for closed-loop electrochemical process screening.
