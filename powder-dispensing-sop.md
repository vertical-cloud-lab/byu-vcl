## 1. Description of Work and Scope of SOP

This SOP describes the procedure for preparing powder mixtures containing Aluminum 1100 wire, Silicon powder, and Aluminum-Silicon 10Mg powder. The process involves cutting aluminum wire to specification, measuring precise masses of materials, and preparing test mixtures for laboratory analysis.

**Scope:** This procedure applies to the preparation of powder test mixtures in the Vertical Cloud Lab.

---

## 2. Prequalifications

- Users must have completed general laboratory safety training
- Users must have read and understood the Safety Data Sheets for all materials used
- Users should have basic familiarity with laboratory balances and safety equipment
- Supervisor approval recommended before first independent execution

## 3. Chemicals Used

| Material | Form | Hazard Class | SDS Available |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Aluminum 1100 | Wire | Combustible Solid | Yes |
| Silicon | Powder | Combustible Solid, Dust Hazard | Yes |
| Aluminum-Silicon 10Mg (AlSi10Mg) | Powder | Combustible Solid, Dust Hazard | Yes |

**Storage:** All materials stored in appropriate containers in designated chemical storage area. Powders kept in sealed containers to minimize exposure and dust generation.

## 4. Summary of Physical Hazards

| Hazard | Severity | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Combustible Powder Dust | Medium | Use in ventilated area, avoid generating excessive dust |
| Dust Inhalation | Medium | Wear appropriate respiratory protection when handling powders |
| Sharp Wire Edges | Medium | Handle cut wire with care, use proper cutting tools |
| Repetitive Motion | Low | Take breaks during extended material handling |
| Eye Contact with Dust | Medium | Wear safety glasses |

---

## 5. Summary of Chemical Hazards

- **Aluminum 1100 (Wire):** Combustible solid; dust is combustible; may generate hydrogen gas in reactive environments; skin and eye irritant in powder form
- **Silicon Powder:** Combustible solid; generates dust hazard; irritant to eyes and respiratory system
- **Aluminum-Silicon 10Mg Powder:** Combustible alloy powder; generates significant dust hazard; irritant to respiratory system and eyes

All materials should be handled to minimize dust generation.

---

## 6. Equipment Required to Perform Work

- **Cutting Tool:** Hacksaw or appropriate wire cutting tool
- **Balance:** Precision balance (accurate to ±0.01 g minimum)
- **Glass Container:** Clean glass jar or beaker for holding mixture
- **Weighing Vessels:** Clean weighing boats or containers for individual materials
- **Measuring Tools:** Rulers or calipers (for wire length measurement)
- **Cleaning Supplies:** Soft brushes, cleaning wipes, waste containers

---

## 7. Engineering Controls

1. **Ventilation:** Perform work in well-ventilated area or use fume hood when handling loose powders
2. **Dust Control:** Keep powders in sealed containers when not actively measuring
3. **Work Surface:** Use smooth, easily cleanable work surface (stainless steel or ceramic preferred)
4. **Segregation:** Keep combustible materials separated from ignition sources

---

## 8. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**Required for all operations:**
- Safety glasses or face shield
- Lab coat
- Closed-toe shoes

**Required when handling loose powders:**
- Nitrile gloves (powder-free or low-powder)
- Dust mask or respiratory protection (N95 minimum; consult SDS for requirements)

**Recommended:**
- Apron (protects lab coat from powder residue)

## 9. Step-by-Step Procedures for Safe Performance of Work

### **Phase 1: Setup and Preparation**

**Step 1.1 - Workspace Preparation (0:00)**
- Clear and clean work surface
- Ensure adequate ventilation (open windows or use fume hood)
- Gather all required equipment and materials
- Verify balance is calibrated and functional
- Put on all required PPE before beginning

**Step 1.2 - Material Verification**
- Verify Aluminum 1100 wire is at room temperature
- Verify powder containers are sealed and in good condition
- Check that all materials match specifications

### **Phase 2: Aluminum Wire Preparation**

**Step 2.1 - Cutting 1100 Aluminum Wire (0:00 - 0:35)**
- Measure required length of Aluminum 1100 wire using ruler or calipers
- Secure wire in vise or appropriate cutting fixture
- Use hacksaw or wire cutter to cut wire to precise length
- Handle cut wire carefully to avoid sharp edges
- Place cut wire in clean container

**Step 2.2 - Measuring Mass of 1100 Wire (0:35 - 1:38)**
- Place weighing boat on precision balance
- Tare the balance to zero
- Carefully transfer cut aluminum wire to weighing boat
- Record mass of wire (target mass per test plan)
- Remove weighing boat from balance

### **Phase 3: Container Preparation**

**Step 3.1 - Tare with Glass Container (1:38 - 2:11)**
- Place clean, dry glass container on precision balance
- Record tare mass of empty container
- If mixing in the container: ensure container is appropriate for intended use
- Verify container is clean and free of contaminants

### **Phase 4: Powder Measurement**

**Step 4.1 - Measure Mass of Silicon Powder (2:11 - 5:39)**
- Ensure dust mask is properly fitted
- Open Silicon powder container in well-ventilated area
- Using small weighing boat or measuring vessel, carefully dispense Silicon powder
- Minimize dust generation when handling
- Place weighing boat with powder on balance
- Record mass of Silicon powder
- If transferring to container: gently transfer to glass container
- Reseal Silicon powder container immediately

**Step 4.2 - Measure Mass of Aluminum-Silicon 10Mg Powder (5:39 - 10:55)**
- Ensure dust mask is properly fitted
- Open AlSi10Mg powder container in well-ventilated area
- Using small weighing boat or measuring vessel, carefully dispense AlSi10Mg powder
- Minimize dust generation when handling
- Place weighing boat with powder on balance
- Record mass of AlSi10Mg powder
- Transfer to glass container with other materials
- Reseal AlSi10Mg powder container immediately

### **Phase 5: Mixture Finalization**

**Step 5.1 - Homogenization (if required)**
- If mixture needs to be homogenized, use appropriate mixing method:
  - Gentle shaking in sealed container (preferred for safety)
  - Stirring with inert tool (if appropriate for application)
- Minimize dust generation during mixing
- Label container with mixture composition and date

### **Phase 6: Cleanup (10:55+)**

**Step 6.1 - Clean Up Workspace (10:55+)**
- While still wearing PPE, wipe down work surface with damp cloth
- Collect any loose powder residue using damp wipes (do NOT use dry sweeping to avoid dust)
- Dispose of cleanup materials and powder waste in appropriate chemical waste containers
- Return all equipment to proper storage locations
- Clean weighing boats and containers immediately after use

**Step 6.2 - Final Safety Steps**
- Verify powder containers are properly sealed and stored
- Remove PPE in the following order:
  1. Remove gloves
  2. Remove dust mask/respiratory protection
  3. Remove safety glasses
  4. Remove lab coat
- Store PPE appropriately
- Dispose of single-use items in appropriate waste

**Step 6.3 - Documentation**
- Record all measurements and mixture composition in laboratory notebook
- Note any deviations from procedure
- Clean balance area if powder residue present

## 10. "Unwanted Lab Material" Collection Procedures

### Disposal of Powder Waste
- Collect all powder residue in appropriate chemical waste containers
- **DO NOT:** Dispose of powders down drain
- **DO NOT:** Mix different powder types in waste container
- Label waste containers with:
  - Contents (e.g., "Aluminum 1100 Dust Waste")
  - Date collected
  - Your name

### Disposal of Contaminated Materials
- Single-use weighing boats containing residual powder: place in chemical waste
- Wipes and materials contaminated with powder: place in appropriate waste stream
- Damaged or unusable wire: may be placed in scrap metal or chemical waste per supervisor guidance

### Cleanup Waste
- All damp wipes used for cleanup: place in designated waste container
- Any water used for cleanup: may be disposed of per normal water disposal procedures

## 12. Chemical Spill / Release Procedures

### Minor Spill (< 100 g of powder)

1. **Immediate Actions:**
   - Do not panic; calm response is most effective
   - Warn others in immediate area
   - Do not attempt to clean with dry sweeping (creates dust hazard)

2. **Containment:**
   - Ventilate area immediately
   - If possible, use damp cloth or mop to prevent dust
   - Do NOT use compressed air

3. **Cleanup:**
   - Use damp cleanup method (wet wipes or damp mop)
   - Collect residue in appropriate waste container
   - Dispose of cleanup materials in chemical waste

4. **Documentation:**
   - Report incident to supervisor
   - Document in incident log

### Major Spill (> 100 g or uncontrollable powder release)

1. **Immediate Actions:**
   - Evacuate area immediately
   - Alert others in vicinity
   - Turn off any ignition sources in area
   - Contact supervisor and safety officer immediately

2. **Do NOT:**
   - Attempt solo cleanup
   - Generate additional dust
   - Use dry sweeping or compressed air

3. **Emergency Response:**
   - Follow BYU Emergency Procedures
   - Call University Police (801-422-2222) if necessary
   - Notify Risk Management (801-422-4468)

4. **Cleanup:**
   - Only trained personnel proceed with cleanup
   - Use damp cleanup methods exclusively
   - Dispose of all materials in appropriate waste containers

## 14. Additional Notes and Considerations

### Best Practices
- Always verify balance calibration at the beginning of the day
- Prepare clean workspace before starting
- Work methodically and avoid rushing through measurements
- Minimize time that powder containers are open
- Document all measurements clearly during procedure

### Quality Considerations
- Use clean equipment for each measurement to avoid cross-contamination
- Verify balance accuracy periodically
- Take multiple measurements if uncertainty exists
- Store prepared mixtures in sealed, labeled containers

### Safety Considerations
- Never work alone when handling hazardous materials
- Keep safety data sheets accessible during procedure
- Report any exposure incidents to supervisor immediately
- Review procedure before each use if first time or after extended break
