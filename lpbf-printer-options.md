# Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) Printer Options

## ASTM E8 Tensile Specimen Requirements

The standard test for metallic tensile specimens is **ASTM E8/E8M**. Key specimen sizes:

| Specimen Type | Gauge Length | Overall Length | Width/Diameter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard round | 50 mm | ~200 mm | 12.5 mm dia |
| Sub-size round | 25 mm | ~100 mm | 6 mm dia |
| Sub-size flat | 25 mm | ~100 mm | 6 mm wide |

**Minimum build volume needed:** At least **100 × 25 × 25 mm** for sub-size specimens printed flat (most compact option), or **~250 × 25 × 25 mm** for full-size standard specimens. Most LPBF printers with a build plate ≥100 mm in one direction can accommodate sub-size ASTM E8 specimens.

## Recommendations

### Best Value (Used/Refurbished)

1. **Concept Laser Mlab cusing 200R (~$44K–$46K used)** — Lowest entry cost. Build volume (100 × 100 × 100 mm) fits sub-size ASTM E8 specimens. Available now on eBay and Machinio. Risk: discontinued by GE, so parts/support may be limited.

2. **EOS M 100 (~$75K–$100K refurbished)** — EOS sells refurbished units directly with support. Build volume (100 mm dia × 95 mm) fits sub-size specimens. Well-established ecosystem.

3. **SLM 125 (~$75K–$150K refurbished)** — Available with warranty from Langer Engineering. Build volume (125 × 125 × 125 mm) fits sub-size specimens comfortably.

4. **EOS M 290 (~$120K–$180K used)** — If full-size ASTM E8 specimens (~200 mm overall length, 50 mm gauge length) are required, this is the most cost-effective used option. Build plate of 250 × 250 mm easily accommodates full-size tensile bars.

### Best Value (New, for Budget-Conscious Labs)

1. **Mastrex MX-100 (~$39K new)** — Lowest new price point with a surprisingly capable 250 × 250 × 300 mm build volume, 500 W laser, and support for full-size ASTM E8 specimens. Best for initial exploration.

2. **One Click Metal BoldSeries (~$100K–$120K new)** — Safe powder cartridge system ideal for university labs. 150 × 150 × 150 mm build volume fits sub-size specimens.

3. **Xact Metal XM200G (~$160K–$210K new)** — Open material platform with up to 150 × 150 × 290 mm build volume. Good balance of capability and price. Single-phase power simplifies installation.

### For Full-Size ASTM E8 Specimens

If printing standard (not sub-size) ASTM E8 tensile bars (~200 mm overall length, 50 mm gauge length) is essential, look for machines with at least 200 mm in one build direction:

- **Mastrex MX-100** (250 × 250 × 300 mm) — new ~$39K
- **EOS M 290** (250 × 250 × 325 mm) — used ~$120K–$180K
- **SLM 280** (280 × 280 × 365 mm) — used ~$150K–$250K
- **Renishaw AM 250** (250 × 250 × 300 mm) — used ~$80K–$150K
- **Xact Metal XM300G** (300 × 300 × 400 mm) — new ~$375K–$450K (available H2 2026)

## Specifications to Discuss with Vendors

When requesting quotes (new or refurbished), specify or ask about the following:

### Materials and Process

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Target alloys** | Which alloys are qualified on this system? (e.g., 316L SS, Ti-6Al-4V, AlSi10Mg, IN718) | Determines if the machine meets your research needs |
| **Open vs. locked materials** | Can we use third-party powders and develop custom parameters? | Critical for research flexibility; locked systems restrict you to OEM powders at higher cost |
| **Powder particle size range** | What PSD is recommended? (typically 15–45 µm or 15–63 µm) | Affects surface finish, density, and powder sourcing |
| **Layer thickness range** | Min/max layer thickness? (typically 20–100 µm) | Thinner layers = better resolution but slower builds |
| **Atmosphere** | Argon or nitrogen? What O₂ level is maintained? | Reactive metals (Ti, Al) require argon; steels can use nitrogen |
| **Gas consumption** | Estimated inert gas usage per build and during idle/purge? | Ongoing operating cost; argon ~$30–$50 per tank |

### Laser and Optics

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Laser power** | Wattage? (e.g., 100 W, 200 W, 400 W, 500 W+) | Higher power = faster scanning, wider material range |
| **Number of lasers** | Single or multi-laser? | Multi-laser increases throughput but adds cost |
| **Spot size** | Focused beam diameter? (typically 50–100 µm) | Affects minimum feature size and surface quality |
| **Scanning speed** | Max scan speed? (typically 1–10 m/s) | Impacts build time |

### Facility Requirements

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Electrical** | Voltage, phase (single vs. 3-phase), amperage, total kW? | Determines if existing lab power is sufficient; 3-phase may require an electrician |
| **Floor space & weight** | Machine footprint and weight? Separate sieving station needed? | Space planning; some units need reinforced flooring |
| **Ventilation/exhaust** | Exhaust requirements? HEPA/activated carbon filtration included? | EHS compliance; metal powder is a respirable hazard |
| **Compressed air** | Required? What pressure and flow rate? | Some systems require clean dry air supply |
| **Cooling** | Chiller required? Included or separate? | Laser cooling is essential; chillers add cost and space |

### Powder Handling and Safety

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Powder loading/unloading** | Manual or automated? Inert atmosphere handling? | Safety with reactive powders (Ti, Al); ease of material changes |
| **Sieving system** | Integrated or separate sieving station? | Powder recycling workflow; some need a separate unit (~$10K–$30K) |
| **Material changeover time** | How long to switch between alloys? | Important if running multiple materials for research |
| **PPE requirements** | What PPE is required for operators? | Respirators, gloves, ESD-safe clothing for reactive powders |

### Software and Data

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Build preparation software** | What CAM/slicing software is included? (e.g., EOSPRINT, Materialise Magics, 3DXpert) | Workflow integration; some require separate licenses |
| **Parameter access** | Can we view/edit scan strategy, laser parameters, support structures? | Open parameters are essential for research; some OEMs lock these |
| **Licensing model** | Perpetual license or annual subscription? Per-seat? | Ongoing cost; subscriptions can be $5K–$15K/yr |
| **File formats** | STL, 3MF, CLI, other? | Compatibility with existing CAD/simulation tools |
| **In-situ monitoring** | Melt pool monitoring, thermal imaging, or layer imaging available? | Valuable for research; may be add-on modules |

### Service, Support, and Training

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Warranty** | Duration and scope? What's covered? | Typically 1 year; laser source warranties may differ |
| **Service contracts** | Annual maintenance cost? What's included? | Can be 5–15% of purchase price per year |
| **Response time** | On-site service response time? Remote diagnostics available? | Downtime cost; remote diagnostics can resolve many issues quickly |
| **Training** | Included? Duration? On-site or at vendor facility? For how many people? | Operator training is critical; typically 3–5 days |
| **Spare parts** | Lead time for common consumables and spare parts? | Recoater blades, filters, build plates, laser windows |
| **Academic references** | Other university installations? Can we contact them? | Peer feedback on real-world experience |

### For Used/Refurbished Units Specifically

| Spec | What to Ask | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Laser hours** | Total laser-on hours? Laser source age? | Fiber lasers typically last 50,000–100,000 hours; key cost driver |
| **Build count** | How many builds has the machine completed? | Indicator of overall wear |
| **Maintenance history** | Full service records available? | Identifies potential issues and remaining life |
| **Remaining warranty** | Any transferable OEM warranty? | Refurbished units from OEMs (EOS, SLM) often include 6–12 month warranty |
| **Firmware/software version** | Current version? Can it be updated? | Older firmware may lack features or security updates |
| **Reason for sale** | Why is the previous owner selling? | Upgrade vs. problem unit |
| **Included accessories** | Build plates, powder, sieving station, chiller, software licenses? | Can save $10K–$50K+ if included |

### Estimated Annual Operating Costs

| Item | Typical Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Inert gas (argon) | $2K–$5K/yr | Depends on build frequency and chamber size |
| Metal powder | $50–$400/kg | 316L SS ~$50–$80/kg; Ti-6Al-4V ~$200–$400/kg; IN718 ~$80–$150/kg |
| Filters (HEPA, gas) | $1K–$3K/yr | Replacement frequency depends on usage |
| Recoater blades | $500–$2K/yr | Consumable; varies by material abrasiveness |
| Build plates | $200–$800 each | Can be resurfaced several times |
| Software licenses | $0–$15K/yr | Depends on licensing model |
| Service contract | $10K–$60K/yr | 5–15% of system purchase price |

### Academic Pricing

When requesting quotes, mention:
- **Academic/educational institution** — most OEMs offer 10–30% discounts for universities
- **Multi-unit or department-wide purchases** — may unlock additional discounts
- **Grant funding** — mention if purchasing through NSF MRI, DoD DURIP, or DOE EPSCoR (vendors are familiar with these programs)
- **Demo/beta units** — ask if any demo, showroom, or beta-test units are available at reduced cost
