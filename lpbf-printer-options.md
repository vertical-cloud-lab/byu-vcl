# Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) Printer Options

## ASTM E8 Tensile Specimen Requirements

The standard test for metallic tensile specimens is **ASTM E8/E8M**. Key specimen sizes:

| Specimen Type | Gauge Length | Overall Length | Width/Diameter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard round | 50 mm | ~200 mm | 12.5 mm dia |
| Sub-size round | 25 mm | ~100 mm | 6 mm dia |
| Sub-size flat | 25 mm | ~100 mm | 6 mm wide |

**Minimum build volume needed:** At least **100 × 25 × 25 mm** for sub-size specimens printed flat (most compact option), or **~250 × 25 × 25 mm** for full-size standard specimens. Most LPBF printers with a build plate ≥100 mm in one direction can accommodate sub-size ASTM E8 specimens.

## Recommendations

### Best Value (Used/Refurbished)

1. **Concept Laser Mlab cusing 200R (~$44K–$46K used)** — Lowest entry cost. Build volume (100 × 100 × 100 mm) fits sub-size ASTM E8 specimens. Available now on eBay and Machinio. Risk: discontinued by GE, so parts/support may be limited.

2. **EOS M 100 (~$75K–$100K refurbished)** — EOS sells refurbished units directly with support. Build volume (100 mm dia × 95 mm) fits sub-size specimens. Well-established ecosystem.

3. **SLM 125 (~$75K–$150K refurbished)** — Available with warranty from Langer Engineering. Build volume (125 × 125 × 125 mm) fits sub-size specimens comfortably.

4. **EOS M 290 (~$120K–$180K used)** — If full-size ASTM E8 specimens (~200 mm overall length, 50 mm gauge length) are required, this is the most cost-effective used option. Build plate of 250 × 250 mm easily accommodates full-size tensile bars.

### Best Value (New, for Budget-Conscious Labs)

1. **Mastrex MX-100 (~$39K new)** — Lowest new price point. Desktop form factor. Best for initial exploration if sub-size specimens are acceptable.

2. **One Click Metal BoldSeries (~$100K–$120K new)** — Safe powder cartridge system ideal for university labs. 150 × 150 × 150 mm build volume fits sub-size specimens.

3. **Xact Metal XM200G (~$160K–$210K new)** — Open material platform with up to 150 × 150 × 290 mm build volume. Good balance of capability and price. Single-phase power simplifies installation.

### For Full-Size ASTM E8 Specimens

If printing standard (not sub-size) ASTM E8 tensile bars (~200 mm overall length, 50 mm gauge length) is essential, look for machines with at least 200 mm in one build direction:

- **EOS M 290** (250 × 250 × 325 mm) — used ~$120K–$180K
- **SLM 280** (280 × 280 × 365 mm) — used ~$150K–$250K
- **Renishaw AM 250** (250 × 250 × 300 mm) — used ~$80K–$150K
- **Xact Metal XM300G** (300 × 300 × 400 mm) — new ~$375K–$450K (available H2 2026)
