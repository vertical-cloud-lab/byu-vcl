## 🚨 CRITICAL: Drainage Solution for Lab Without Floor Drain

**Your Situation**: No drain in the lab, but adjacent lab has a drain.

**✅ RECOMMENDED SOLUTION: Condensate Pump**

All commercial dehumidifiers can use a **condensate pump** to move water to a remote drain. This eliminates manual emptying and enables 24/7 unattended operation.

### Condensate Pump Options:

**Built-in Pumps** (Available on some models):
- Can pump water **15-20 feet vertically** or **100+ feet horizontally**
- Uses small tubing (3/8" or 1/2") that can route through walls, ceiling, or under doors
- **Automatic operation** - no intervention needed
- **Cost**: Often built-in or $100-300 add-on

**External Condensate Pumps** (Universal solution):
- **Little Giant VCMA-15ULS** (~$150): 15 ft lift, reliable
- **Hartell GPM-2** (~$180): 20 ft lift, commercial grade  
- **Liberty Pumps LCU-20** (~$200): High capacity, 20 ft lift

### For Your Lab:
1. Install dehumidifier with gravity drain port
2. Connect to condensate pump
3. Run 3/8" tubing through wall/ceiling to adjacent lab's drain
4. **No manual emptying required** ✅

**⚠️ AVOID Manual Bucket Emptying**: At 100+ pints/day capacity:
- **Quest 155**: 155 pints/day = **19 gallons/day** = Empty ~38 times/day with 1-gallon bucket
- Manual emptying is **NOT practical** for continuous operation

### Best Pumping Solutions by Model:
- **Quest 155**: Built-in drain port, add external pump ($150-200)
- **Aprilaire 1870**: Drain port included, add external pump  
- **AlorAir HD55/HD90**: Built-in pump option available (WiFi models)
- **Santa Fe Ultra V155**: Gravity drain + optional pump kit

## Installation Considerations

### For Your Lab (No Floor Drain):

1. **Drainage Solution - CRITICAL**:
   - ✅ **Install condensate pump** to route water to adjacent lab's drain
   - Run 3/8" or 1/2" tubing through wall/ceiling/under door
   - Pumps can lift water 15-20 feet vertically or 100+ feet horizontally
   - **Recommended pumps**: Little Giant VCMA-15ULS ($150), Hartell GPM-2 ($180)
   - Budget $150-200 for external pump + $50-100 for tubing/installation

2. **Electrical**: 
   - All recommended models use standard 115-120V
   - Verify dedicated 15A circuit at installation location
   - Quest 155: 6.9A, AlorAir HD55: ~7.5A

3. **Airflow**: 
   - Ensure adequate clearance for air intake/exhaust (12-18" minimum)
   - **For ventilated room**: Freestanding units (Quest 155, AlorAir) are better than ducted
   - Avoid blocking existing room ventilation

4. **Ducting Considerations**: 
   - **NOT recommended** for your already-ventilated room
   - Ducted systems (Aprilaire 1870) interfere with existing ventilation
   - Freestanding units work better in ventilated spaces

5. **Placement**: Consider proximity to:
   - Sensitive equipment requiring humidity control
   - Electrical outlets (within 9 feet for Quest 155's power cord)
   - Route to adjacent lab for pump discharge
   - Air circulation patterns - don't block vents
