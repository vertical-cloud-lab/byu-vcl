Quantitative compositional analysis of aluminum alloy bulk powder materials, specifically AlSi10Mg additive manufacturing powder. Cover:
1. Best practices for quantitative EDS (Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy) compositional analysis of Al-Si-Mg alloy powders
2. Challenges in detecting trace Mg (0.2-0.45 wt%) in an Al matrix using SEM-EDS - detection limits, peak overlap between Mg Kα (1.253 keV) and Al Kα (1.486 keV)
3. Alternative/complementary techniques for verifying composition of gas-atomized Al alloy powders (ICP-OES, XRF, WDS vs EDS)
4. Standards-based vs standardless quantification accuracy for light elements (Mg, Al, Si) in SEM-EDS
5. Sample preparation effects on quantitative accuracy for powder samples (slurry vs carbon tape mounting)
6. ZAF/phi-rho-z matrix corrections relevant to Al-Si-Mg system
7. How surface oxide layers and Mg surface segregation in gas-atomized powder affect EDS quantification vs bulk composition
