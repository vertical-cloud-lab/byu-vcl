# DRAFT — Open Questions for BYU Offices

> Agenda for the first Regulatory Accounting meeting (plus the SPO / Treasury /
> Tax Office / Fixed Assets questions to route in parallel). Ordered so the
> answers unblock the establishment and rate proposals. Page cites refer to the
> *Recharge Center Policy & Procedures* PDF (June 2005) in this repo.

## For Regulatory Accounting (the decision authority)

**Policy currency & process**
1. Is the June 2005 Recharge Center Policy & Procedures still the controlling
   version? Any post-Uniform-Guidance (2 CFR 200) updates, current forms, or a
   proposal template we should use instead of a memo?
2. Who is the current Director of Regulatory Accounting, and what is the
   realistic review timeline to have **rates effective January 1, 2027**?
3. Does the VP office sign the establishment proposal in practice (the
   responsibilities table mentions Chair, Dean's *and* VP's office; §2 narrates
   Chair → Dean → Regulatory Accounting)?

**Depreciation schedule (time-sensitive)**
4. BYU's standard useful life for this equipment class (ultrasonic metal
   atomizer, ~$250k)? We assumed 10 years; would a 5-year life be entertained
   with a technological-obsolescence justification (p. 7)?
5. First-year proration: monthly from the in-service date, or an annual
   convention? (Determines exactly how much of 2026 is forfeited.)
6. Confirm the depreciable base = capitalized acquisition cost including
   **tariff/duty, freight, and installation** (2 CFR 200.1) as booked by Fixed
   Assets — and the process to fix the asset record if ancillary charges were
   expensed instead.
7. Can machine-specific renovation line items (rigging, dedicated electrical,
   inert-gas plumbing, machine-specific ventilation) be **reclassified from the
   building/renovation project to the equipment asset record** before schedule
   approval? What documentation do you need?

**§8 reimbursement**
8. Process and documentation for **prior approval** of reimbursing the startup
   account(s) that funded the equipment (pp. 7–8). Is per-run in-kind credit at
   the internal rate acceptable as the valuation method? Our draft ledger is in
   the equipment package — does it satisfy the documentation requirement?

**Rates & structure**
9. Approval of a per-job **minimum fee** for setup/expendables (p. 4).
10. For **external sales**: what institutional overhead rate and base apply —
    the negotiated research F&A (51.5%?) on the full cost-based price, or
    something else? Where does collected overhead land (any return to
    college/department)?
11. Any constraints on **surcharge magnitude/structure** for non-federal
    externals — specifically a posted, consistently applied **embargo-tier menu**
    (premiums for 6/12/24-month/indefinite data embargoes) riding on the
    surcharge?
12. Our expected mix is **majority-external billed usage** (internal own-lab use
    is large but mostly unbilled/§8). Does that affect the center's
    classification ("primarily to internal University operations," p. 1), UBIT
    review, or anything in how we should scope the proposal?
13. Is a blanket **zero/near-zero internal rate** (gap funded by non-federal
    startup funds, usage still in the denominator, unrecovered costs tracked and
    excluded from F&A proposals, pp. 3–5) acceptable if we choose that
    structure instead of §8 in-kind?
14. The **no-advance-billing** rule (p. 2) vs. a possible future prepaid-credit
    "cloud lab" model — is there any negotiable path (e.g., deposits held as
    unearned revenue), or is payment strictly at/after delivery?
15. Mid-year rate revisions: threshold for "significant" change requiring a new
    proposal (p. 2)?
