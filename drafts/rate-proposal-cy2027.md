## 2. Depreciation

- Annual depreciation: $250,000 ÷ 10 = **$25,000/yr** ($2,083.33/month).
- Equipment purchased entirely with **non-federal startup funds** → depreciation
  includable in rates (Policy pp. 6–7); requires the approved depreciation
  schedule and equipment reserve account requested in the companion package.
- 2026 stub (in-service ~June 2026 → rates effective Jan 1, 2027): ~7 months ×
  $2,083 ≈ **$14.6k of depreciation permanently forfeited** ("rates cannot
  include depreciation from prior years," p. 6). No catch-up is possible; this
  proposal claims CY2027 depreciation only.
- Depreciation component of each billed run is journaled to the **equipment
  reserve account** (Policy p. 7).

## 7. Own-lab usage treatment and unrecovered costs

**The approved internal rate remains the gross, cost-based rate in all cases**
(Edison A4): PI-group usage is recorded run-by-run at that gross rate and then
either charged, covered by a documented non-federal subsidy, or credited under
Policy §8 — never posted as a separate "zero rate."

Preferred election (companion package §3): the PI-group's ~200 non-federal runs
are taken as **§8 in-kind reimbursement** — services without charge, credited
against the documented startup investment, capped at the amount invested, with
prior Regulatory Accounting approval. **The credit valuation (full internal
rate vs. depreciation component only vs. a use-allowance) requires a written
ruling before any repayment timeline is projected** — the earlier "~4 years to
credit $250,000" estimate is withdrawn; see
[`recharge-plan-working-notes.md`](recharge-plan-working-notes.md) §5 for how
far the valuation choice swings the answer (~1.5 years to beyond the useful
life).

Consequences, stated for transparency:

- Those runs stay in the denominator; their share of the pool (200/305 of the
  total — ~$167k/yr under the staffed planning pool) is **not** recovered in
  cash. The startup/department absorbs it (permitted under-recovery from
  non-federal funds, Policy pp. 3–5); it will be tracked and excluded from any
  F&A proposal (p. 4).
- Unbilled runs deposit no depreciation cash into the reserve, so the reserve
  accumulates only from billed volume (~105 runs × $82 ≈ $8.6k/yr under this
  forecast). Reimbursement-vs-reserve tradeoff acknowledged.
- Any own-lab job charged to a **federal award** is billed at the uniform
  internal rate.

## 9. True-up commitment

The center will submit a rate proposal to Regulatory Accounting **at least
annually**, roll any CY2027 surplus/deficit into CY2028 rates, log all usage
whether billed or not, keep depreciation journal entries current, and submit a
mid-year revision if services, costs, or methodology change significantly
(Policy pp. 2–6). In CY2027 the center will additionally review actuals
**quarterly** and trigger a mid-year revision if labor per run differs from
forecast by >20%, utilization by >25%, or the external mix changes materially
(Edison D9 governance triggers — judgment thresholds, not policy requirements).
