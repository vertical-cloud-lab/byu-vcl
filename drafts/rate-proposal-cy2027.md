## 2. Depreciation

- Annual depreciation: $250,000 ÷ 10 = **$25,000/yr** ($2,083.33/month).
- Equipment purchased entirely with **non-federal startup funds** → depreciation
  includable in rates (Policy pp. 6–7); requires the approved depreciation
  schedule and equipment reserve account requested in the companion package.
- 2026 stub (in-service ~June 2026 → rates effective Jan 1, 2027): ~7 months ×
  $2,083 ≈ **$14.6k of depreciation permanently forfeited** ("rates cannot
  include depreciation from prior years," p. 6). No catch-up is possible; this
  proposal claims CY2027 depreciation only.
- Depreciation component of each billed run is journaled to the **equipment
  reserve account** (Policy p. 7).

## 5. Rate derivation

**Internal rate** = $93,375 ÷ 305 runs = **$306/run** (≈ $51/machine-hour).
Depreciation component: $25,000 ÷ 305 ≈ **$82/run** → equipment reserve.

**Proposed rate schedule (CY2027):**

| Tier | $/run | Basis |
|------|-------|-------|
| Internal (all BYU accounts — uniform) | **$306** | Cost (p. 4) |
| External, documented federal funding (award number/approval on file) | **$464** | $306 × 1.515 (cost + institutional overhead, no surcharge, p. 5) |
| External, non-federal — standard (30-day data-release terms) | **$625** | Cost + overhead + surcharge (p. 5) |
| External, non-federal — embargo menu (surcharge tiers; see service agreement) | $775 (+6 mo) / $925 (+12 mo) / $1,225 (+24 mo) / $1,525 (indefinite/proprietary) | Surcharge only — unavailable to federal-documented users |
| Minimum fee, per initiated job (setup + expendables; requested per p. 4) | **$150** | Initiation costs |

Notes: (i) surcharge revenue is tracked separately and used per Policy p. 5
(reduce rates / working capital / equipment purchases); (ii) card-paid external
jobs bear ~2.9% merchant fees — absorbed in the surcharge for non-federal users;
federal-documented externals will be steered to invoice/ACH, with any card fees
treated as center operating cost; (iii) Utah sales tax on tangible product
(powder) shipped to external customers is collected **on top** via Avalara —
not center revenue; (iv) **no advance billing** (p. 2): invoicing at/after
delivery.

## 7. Own-lab usage treatment and unrecovered costs

Preferred election (companion package §3): the PI-group's ~200 non-federal runs
are taken as **§8 in-kind reimbursement** — services without charge, credited
against the documented startup investment at the internal rate
(200 × $306 ≈ **$61,200/yr** of credit → ~4 years to credit $250,000), capped
at the amount invested, with prior Regulatory Accounting approval.

Consequences, stated for transparency:

- Those runs stay in the denominator; their ~$61k share of the pool is **not**
  recovered in cash. The startup/department absorbs it (permitted under-recovery
  from non-federal funds, Policy pp. 3–5); it will be tracked and excluded from
  any F&A proposal (p. 4).
- Unbilled runs deposit no depreciation cash into the reserve, so the reserve
  accumulates only from billed volume (~105 runs × $82 ≈ $8.6k/yr under this
  forecast). Reimbursement-vs-reserve tradeoff acknowledged.
- Any own-lab job charged to a **federal award** is billed at the uniform $306.

## 8. Reasonableness check against published peer rates

Northwestern's CHiMaD Metals Processing Facility (AMAZEMET rePowder) publishes
per-run ultrasonic-atomizer rates of **$1,075 internal / $1,720 external
academic / $2,150 commercial**. Our $306 internal figure is ~3.5× lower, almost
certainly because this draft carries only 2 attended operator-hours per run and
no dedicated staff.

**Scenario B (staffed):** adding 0.5 FTE technician effort at $45,000/yr loaded
raises the pool to $138,375 → internal **$454/run**, federal external
**$688/run** — still below Northwestern. Before submission, replace §4's labor
lines with an honest staffing plan (who operates, trains users, maintains,
schedules; at what loaded rates). Under-costing labor is the most common way
first-year recharge rates fail audit and the fastest way to build an
unrecoverable deficit.

## 9. True-up commitment

The center will submit a rate proposal to Regulatory Accounting **at least
annually**, roll any CY2027 surplus/deficit into CY2028 rates, log all usage
whether billed or not, keep depreciation journal entries current, and submit a
mid-year revision if services, costs, or methodology change significantly
(Policy pp. 2–6).
