## 2. Depreciation

- Annual depreciation: $250,000 ÷ 5 = **$50,000/yr** ($4,166.67/month),
  straight-line, no residual.
- **Five-year life — prior approval requested (Policy p. 7).** Justification:
  near-continuous duty cycle (forecast ~1,800+ machine-hours/yr), consumable-
  intensive ultrasonic hardware (sonotrode/crucible thermal cycling), and the
  pace of technological change in metal-AM powder production equipment.
- Equipment purchased entirely with **non-federal startup funds** → depreciation
  includable in rates (Policy pp. 6–7); requires the approved depreciation
  schedule and equipment reserve account requested in the companion equipment
  package.
- 2026 stub (in-service ~June 2026 → rates effective Jan 1, 2027): ~7 months ×
  $4,167 ≈ **$29.2k of depreciation not recovered** ("rates cannot include
  depreciation from prior years," p. 6). No catch-up is possible; this
  proposal claims CY2027 depreciation only. The schedule ends mid-2031.
- Depreciation component of each billed run (**$164**) is journaled to the
  **equipment reserve account** (Policy p. 7).

## 5. Proposed rate schedule

**Internal rate** = $280,075 ÷ 305 runs = **$918/run** (≈ $153/machine-hour).
Depreciation component: $50,000 ÷ 305 ≈ **$164/run** → equipment reserve.

| Tier | $/run | Basis |
|------|-------|-------|
| Internal (all BYU accounts — uniform) | **$918** | Cost (p. 4) |
| External, documented federal funding (award number/approval on file) | **$1,391** | $918 × 1.515 (cost + institutional overhead, no surcharge, p. 5) |
| External, non-federal | **$1,650** | Cost + overhead + $259 surcharge (p. 5) |
| Minimum fee, per initiated job (setup + expendables; requested per p. 4) | **$150** | Initiation costs |

**Data-embargo fees** (non-federal external users only — an embargo fee is a
surcharge and therefore unavailable to federally documented users, p. 5). Run
data is otherwise released publicly under a permissive license after a 3-month
grace period (see service agreement §5); the embargo delays that release:

| Embargo term | Added fee |
|---|---|
| 6 months | +$1,000 |
| 12 months | +$2,500 |
| 24 months | +$5,000 |
| 48 months | +$10,000 |

There is no indefinite-confidentiality tier; every embargo expires, and work
requiring permanent confidentiality is routed to a sponsored-research
agreement.

Notes: (i) surcharge revenue (including embargo fees) is tracked separately
and used per Policy p. 5 (reduce rates / working capital / equipment
purchases); (ii) credit-card acceptance only through BYU Treasury-approved
channels (eMarkets); no lab-owned processors; card-paid non-federal jobs bear
~2.9% merchant fees absorbed in the surcharge; federal-documented externals
are steered to invoice/ACH, and if one pays by card the fee is a center
operating cost recycled through the pool — never a per-transaction add-on;
(iii) Utah sales tax on tangible product (powder) shipped to external
customers is collected **on top** via Avalara — not center revenue; (iv) **no
advance billing** (p. 2): invoicing at/after delivery.

## 7. Own-lab usage treatment and unrecovered costs

**The approved internal rate remains the gross, cost-based rate in all
cases:** PI-group usage is recorded run-by-run at that gross rate and then
settled — never posted as a separate "zero rate."

Settlement election (companion package §5): the PI-group's ~200 non-federal
runs are taken as **§8 in-kind reimbursement** — services without charge,
credited against the documented startup investment, capped at the amount
invested, with prior Regulatory Accounting approval, including a written
determination of the per-run credit value.

Consequences, stated for transparency:

- Those runs stay in the denominator; their share of the pool (200/305 ≈
  $184k/yr) is **not** recovered in cash. The startup/department absorbs it
  (permitted under-recovery from non-federal funds, Policy pp. 3–5); it is
  tracked and excluded from any F&A proposal (p. 4).
- Unbilled runs deposit no depreciation cash into the reserve, so the reserve
  accumulates only from billed volume (~105 runs × $164 ≈ $17.2k/yr under this
  forecast).
- Any own-lab job charged to a **federal award** is billed at the uniform
  internal rate.

## 8. Reasonableness check against published peer rates

Northwestern's CHiMaD Metals Processing Facility operates the same instrument
class (AMAZEMET rePowder) and publishes per-run ultrasonic-atomizer rates of
**$1,075 internal / $1,720 external academic / $2,150 commercial**. The
proposed schedule ($918 / $1,391 / $1,650) sits below Northwestern at every
tier — consistent with a newer, lower-overhead operation. Other published
academic AM-facility comparators: University of Washington ME (unbundled
machine-hour + powder + engineer-hour rates), Georgia Tech AMPF, UMass ADDFab,
LSU AMMF.

The staffing line (A10) is the largest single element of the pool and the one
the July–August time study finalizes. For transparency, the internal rate at
alternative staffing levels: 1.0 FTE → $623/run; 1.5 FTE → $771/run; 2.0 FTE
(proposed) → $918/run. The bare floor with no dedicated staffing — student
operator hours only — computes to $388/run and is shown only to document the
pool floor; it is not a proposable rate for a customer-facing facility.

## 9. True-up commitment

The center will submit a rate proposal to Regulatory Accounting **at least
annually**, roll any CY2027 surplus/deficit into CY2028 rates, log all usage
whether billed or not, keep depreciation journal entries current, and submit a
mid-year revision if services, costs, or methodology change significantly
(Policy pp. 2–6). In CY2027 the center will additionally review actuals
**quarterly** and trigger a mid-year revision if labor per run differs from
forecast by >20%, utilization by >25%, or the external mix changes materially.
