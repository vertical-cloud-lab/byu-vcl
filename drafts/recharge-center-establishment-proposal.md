## 1. Purpose and mission relationship

The VCL operates a research-grade **ultrasonic metal atomizer** (AMAZEMET
rePowder-class system, acquired [MONTH] 2026 for approximately $250,000
*(confirm capitalized cost)*) producing spherical metal powders (initially
aluminum alloys, e.g., AlSi10Mg) for additive manufacturing research. Demand for
runs on this instrument comes from:

- the PI's own research group (near-continuous baseline use);
- other BYU research groups (occasional);
- external academic researchers, primarily US and primarily federally funded;
- external industrial users (occasional), potentially including
  distributed-manufacturing network partners.

Operating the instrument as a recharge center (a) provides an auditable,
uniform, cost-based mechanism to charge users as required by federal cost
principles; (b) recovers equipment depreciation into an equipment reserve for
eventual replacement; and (c) directly supports the department's educational
mission — student operators run the instrument, and the remote-access "cloud
lab" infrastructure built around it is itself a subject of student research and
publication.

The center's services are directly and substantially related to the University's
research and educational mission (Policy p. 1): powder production for AM
research, student training in metals processing, and published research on
remote-access laboratory operation.

**Disclosure per Policy definition of a recharge center** ("provides services
primarily to internal University operations and secondarily to external users,"
p. 1): while the PI's own (internal) usage is expected to be the largest single
share, the external share of *billed* usage may be substantial. We flag this
mix explicitly and request Regulatory Accounting's guidance on proper scoping,
including UBIT and sales-tax review for external sales (Policy §5).

## 4. Rate methodology (summary — full detail in attached rate proposal)

Per Policy §4: **Annual Rate = Annual Costs ÷ Total Annual Usage.**

- Cost pool includes: straight-line depreciation of the atomizer (subject to an
  approved depreciation schedule — see attached equipment package), maintenance,
  operating supplies (argon, sonotrodes, crucibles), operator labor (student
  wages + benefits), and allocable administrative support. Only allowable,
  allocable, reasonable, consistently treated costs; nothing recovered through
  the F&A rate; no building depreciation/rent/O&M (Policy p. 3).
- All internal users pay one uniform, cost-based rate; internal rates are set to
  break even over five years; surpluses/deficits roll into the following year's
  rates (Policy pp. 3–6).
- External users pay cost + institutional overhead; externals documenting
  federal funding pay no surcharge; a surcharge is added for non-University,
  non-federal users, to be used per Policy p. 5 (rate reduction, working
  capital, equipment purchases).
- No advance billing (Policy p. 2). External invoices at/after delivery;
  optional card payment through Treasury Services' eMarkets program.

## 7. Own-lab usage and subsidy structure

The PI's group's usage will be handled by one of the following (decision
pending Regulatory Accounting guidance; all options keep the runs in the usage
denominator per Policy p. 4):

- **Option A (preferred):** in-kind reimbursement under Policy §8 — the startup
  account that funded the equipment receives services without charge, credited
  against the documented investment, capped at the amount invested.
- **Option B:** pay the uniform internal rate from startup (non-federal) funds.
- **Option C:** center elects to cover specific internal usage from non-federal,
  non-recharge funds (Policy p. 5), with unrecovered costs tracked and excluded
  from F&A proposals.

Any own-lab job charged to a **federal award** will be billed at the uniform
internal rate (Options A/C apply only to non-federally-funded usage).
