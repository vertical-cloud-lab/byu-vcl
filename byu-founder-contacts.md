# BYU Faculty-Founder Precedents and Contacts

People at (or from) BYU — weighted toward the Ira A. Fulton College of Engineering — who have personally navigated the faculty/startup/VC boundary described in [BYU rules on VC money and founder involvement](byu-vc-founder-rules.md), plus the administrators and ecosystem figures who govern or fund that boundary. Compiled July 2026 for the Vertical Cloud Lab (VCL); see [PR #156](https://github.com/vertical-cloud-lab/byu-vcl/pull/156).

> **A networking list, not an endorsement list.** Roles were verified against public sources linked inline, but people move; confirm titles via the BYU directory before reaching out. Contact details are deliberately given as public directory/office links rather than personal emails.

## Tier 2 — College of Engineering faculty who founded or licensed to companies

By department, most VCL-relevant first:

### Chemical Engineering

- **Larry Baxter** — co-founded **Sustainable Energy Solutions** (2008) around his cryogenic carbon capture research; ran it alongside his professorship for over a decade with major DOE/NETL funding until Chart Industries [acquired SES in December 2020](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2020/12/23/2149879/0/en/chart-industries-acquires-sustainable-energy-solutions-inc.html) ($20M + up to $25M earn-out). The closest engineering analogue to what a VCL faculty founder would do: hardware/process company, federal grants, faculty role retained throughout. ([BYU News](https://news.byu.edu/news/byu-technology-tackles-climate-change-freezing-carbon), [Y Magazine](https://magazine.byu.edu/article/stopping-co2-cold/))

### Mechanical Engineering

- **Anton Bowden** — twice through the pipeline: co-inventor (with Howell and alum Peter Halverson) of the Nexus Spine disc-replacement technology, and co-inventor of the smartfoam licensed to **XO-NANO** ([BYU News](https://news.byu.edu/news/byu-developed-helmet-smartfoam-signals-potential-concussions-real-time)). ([directory](https://www.me.byu.edu/directory/anton-bowden))
- **David Fullwood** — co-inventor on the XO-NANO smartfoam license; experienced in the faculty-inventor-to-student-founder handoff (his PhD student Jake Merrell founded the company).
- **Historic ME:** Tracy Nelson & Carl Sorensen (**MegaStir Technologies**, friction-stir welding) — see [Y Magazine "Entreprofessors"](https://magazine.byu.edu/article/entreprofessors/).

### Electrical & Computer Engineering

- **Randy Beard** — co-developed the Procerus-licensed UAV autopilot technology with McLain; still active BYU faculty. ([Entreprofessors](https://magazine.byu.edu/article/entreprofessors/))
- **Karl Warnick** — co-founded **Linear Signal, LLC** (phased-array antennas), which *funded a BYU research project* to commercialize the technology — direct, personal experience with the touchiest COI shape VCL will face: a founder's company sponsoring the founder's own lab. ([ECE directory](https://ece.byu.edu/directory/karl-warnick))
- **David Long** — didn't found IMSAR, but his radar lab produced it (founder Ryan Smith's master's thesis became the NanoSAR); the model of a professor whose students spin out while he stays purely academic. ([ECE directory](https://ece.byu.edu/directory/david-long), [IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/winner-radio-eye-in-the-sky))
- **Historic ECE:** Doug Chabries & Richard Christiansen (**Sonic Innovations**, digital hearing aids — IPO'd company) — emeritus. ([Entreprofessors](https://magazine.byu.edu/article/entreprofessors/))

### Civil & Construction Engineering

- **Norm Jones** and **Jim Nelson** — built water-modeling software (GMS/SMS/WMS) in BYU's Environmental Modeling Research Laboratory, then in 2007 **moved development off campus into Aquaveo, LLC**, which they co-founded and which still operates in Provo — while both remain full professors. The cleanest local template for VCL's "university lab develops it, company productizes it" software split, and a direct example of the resource-wall rule (see [rules doc](byu-vc-founder-rules.md) §3) driving a company's structure. ([Jones CV](https://cce.byu.edu/00000189-4712-d1e3-a1db-67bb64ce0001/jones-cv-2023), [Entreprofessors](https://magazine.byu.edu/article/entreprofessors/))
- **Michael Scott** — **Coreform** (simulation software from his licensed research); holds the policy-favored "senior advisor" (non-officer) role. Already profiled in the [rules doc](byu-vc-founder-rules.md#precedents-at-byu). ([BYU News](https://news.byu.edu/byu-tech-leads-to-one-of-the-fastest-growing-companies-in-america))
- **Historic CCE:** David W. Jensen (**IsoTruss** composite structures). ([Entreprofessors](https://magazine.byu.edu/article/entreprofessors/))

## Tier 3 — alumni founders (the company-side perspective)

People who took BYU engineering research out and built the company — useful for the view from the other side of the license:

- **David Arnold** — the "leave and run it" path personified: eight years as a BYU ECE professor, then left in 2000 to be founding CEO of **Wavetronix**. The complement to Michael Jensen's stay-on-faculty path *within the same company*: talking to both would map the whole decision. ([ECE honored-alumnus profile](https://ece.byu.edu/content/dr-david-arnold-recognized-2013-honored-alumnus-0))
- **Ryan Smith** and **Logan Harris** — founded/lead **IMSAR** (2004), the SAR company grown from Smith's BYU master's work; both ex-Wavetronix. ([IMSAR about](https://www.imsar.com/about))
- **Jake Merrell** — BYU ME PhD → founder/president of **XO-NANO Smartfoam**, licensee of his own graduate-work IP; the student-founder path VCL's students might follow. ([BYU News](https://news.byu.edu/xonano-smartfoam-was-developed-at-byu-when-lead-engineer-jake-merrill-was-a-graduate-student-here))
- **Peter Halverson** (BYU PhD, Nexus Spine co-inventor/engineer) and **David Hawkes** (BYU MBA, Nexus Spine CEO) — ran the Crocker-funded commercialization of Howell/Bowden's technology. ([Utah Business](https://www.utahbusiness.com/spine-implant-innovation-nexus/))
- **Jeff Jenkins** (Marriott School, not engineering) — the strongest *equity-holding* faculty-founder precedent (NeuroID, through Experian acquisition); profiled in the [rules doc](byu-vc-founder-rules.md#precedents-at-byu).

## What to ask them

1. **Structure:** advisory vs. officer vs. leave — what did they choose, and would they choose it again? (Arnold vs. Jensen at Wavetronix is a natural paired case study.)
2. **Approvals in practice:** how the Outside Activities disclosure, chair/dean sign-off, and (where applicable) COI management plans actually played out — timeline, friction, surprises.
3. **The sponsor-founder loop:** for Warnick and Baxter specifically — how BYU papered their company funding research in their own or colleagues' labs, and how students were protected.
4. **TTO deal shape:** license terms, whether BYU took equity, how the ≥10% royalty-forfeiture rule affected their cap table decisions (see [rules doc](byu-vc-founder-rules.md#3-co-founder-with-monetary-ties--equity)).
5. **For Howell/Alder/McLain:** what a VCL-friendly structure would need to look like for their offices to approve it readily.
